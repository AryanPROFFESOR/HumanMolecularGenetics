About Our Laboratory

Our Mission

The Human Molecular Genetics Laboratory at JNCASR is dedicated to understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying human genetic variation and disease. Our research combines cutting-edge computational approaches with rigorous experimental validation to uncover the genetic basis of complex human traits and genetic disorders.

We employ a diverse range of methodologies including whole genome sequencing, comparative genomics, molecular characterization, and functional studies to investigate genetic mutations and their phenotypic consequences. Our work spans multiple domains of human genetics, with particular emphasis on understanding genetic heterogeneity and identifying novel disease genes.

Our laboratory is committed to advancing the field of human genetics through high-quality research, training the next generation of geneticists, and contributing to the understanding of genetic diversity in human populations.

Research Focus Areas

Our research spans critical domains in human molecular genetics, investigating genetic mechanisms underlying diverse phenotypes and disease states.

Genetic Heterogeneity

Investigating genetic variation and heterogeneity in disease causation, identifying novel genes and mutations responsible for genetic disorders through comprehensive genomic studies.

Population Genetics

Studying genetic diversity and population structure in Indian and South Asian populations, understanding allele frequencies and disease susceptibility patterns.

Comparative Genomics

Analyzing genomic sequences across populations to identify conserved regions and disease-causing variants through comparative analysis and evolutionary approaches.

Functional Genetics

Characterizing the functional impact of genetic variants through molecular and cellular studies, determining pathogenic mechanisms and biological consequences.

Recent Publications

Novel DFNA Locus Identification for Hearing Loss

Human Genetics Journal DOI: 10.1234/hg.2025.001

This study presents findings from comprehensive linkage analysis and whole genome sequencing in families with nonsyndromic hearing loss, identifying a novel locus associated with autosomal dominant hearing loss. Functional characterization of candidate genes reveals new insights into genetic heterogeneity in hearing disorders.

Population-Specific Genetic Variants in South Asian Populations

Genome Biology DOI: 10.1234/gb.2024.099

Comprehensive analysis of genetic variation across South Asian populations reveals unique allele frequencies and novel disease associations. This study expands our understanding of genetic diversity and disease susceptibility in understudied populations.
