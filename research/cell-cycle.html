Cell Cycle & Ciliopathies

Cell Cycle Regulation

We are interested in understanding the mechanisms employed by proteins involved in cell cycle regulation, ciliogenesis, apoptosis and their links to pathophysiology of various neurological disorders with a focus on genetic generalized epilepsy. Genetic analysis on cohorts of epileptic and control individuals from the Indian population has yielded a set of clinically relevant mutations which are being assayed through various cellular and animal models for their effects on cell cycle and its links to the brain.

RNA Binding Factors in Cilia Biology and Ciliopathies

Cilia are highly specialized organelles essential for numerous cellular functions, including signal transduction, fluid movement, and embryonic development. Defects in the formation or function of cilia lead to a group of disorders collectively known as ciliopathies, often characterized by developmental anomalies affecting multiple organ systems. Recent research has highlighted the emerging role of RNA-binding factors in regulating cilia biogenesis and function.

These proteins are crucial for precise RNA processing, particularly alternative splicing of genes essential for ciliogenesis and ciliary signaling. Misregulation or mutations in such factors can disrupt the splicing of ciliary genes, leading to abnormal cilia structure or signaling defects, and ultimately contribute to the pathogenesis of ciliopathies.

Understanding the molecular interplay between RNA processing machinery and ciliary pathways offers new insights into the etiology of these complex disorders and opens avenues for potential therapeutic strategies.
